Step 2: Navigate to the Email Signature Area in Outlook Settings

Now that you have your chosen signature template copied, we're going to open Outlook to set it up. Open up Outlook, then navigate to Settings > Mail > Compose and Reply > Signature (access this area by clicking the Settings cog in the top right corner of your screen.


Visual guide:

Open Outlook, and locate the Settings cog in the top right. Click on it to expand an options menu.


Scroll down to the very bottom of the options menu that has appeared on the right-hand side of your screen. Click on the link that says "View All Outlook Settings"

In the Settings panel that appears, click on the section 'Mail' and the sub-section 'Compose and Reply'. This is where we'll be inputting the email signature you copied.

Step 3: Paste Template into Signature Panel & Modify With Your Own Information

Simply paste the copied signature into the Email Signature text area. It will retain its formatting once pasted, and you can simply edit the Name, Position Title, and/or Phone Number fields to display your own contact info.

When you are done entering this information, press the SAVE button in the lower right corner of the settings panel - then close this panel using the close button in the top right corner.



Step 4: Test It Out!

Send a test email to yourself to be sure that the signature has been activated properly. If so - you're good to go! :)

Please note - a memo about mobile devices: Complex signatures like this are NOT avaialble on mobile devices. When you send an email from Outlook on your phone, this signature will NOT be appended to the end of each email like it is when using Outlook on a computer. If you want to ensure that your contact information is also displayed in an email signature when you are using the Outlook app on iOS or Android, navigate to the settings area of these apps to locate where to change your mobile email signature:

On iOS: access the settings by clicking your profile icon in the top left. Then click the settings cog at the bottom of the panel that appears, and find the 'Signature' settings configuration. Here, you will be able to input a simple text-only signature. Write your new email signature into the text box provided, and then tap the tick in the top right corner to save the signature.

On Android: tap the three-line menu bar in the top left. Then click the settings cog at the bottom of the panel that appears, and find the 'Signature' settings configuration. Here, you will be able to input a simple text-only signature. Write your new email signature into the text box provided, and then tap the tick in the top right corner to save the signature.
